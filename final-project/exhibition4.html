Yahoo data breach (2013-2014)

In 2013, Hackers gained access to the user database through phishing attacks or by exploiting vulnerabilities in Yahoo's systems.

The leaked data includes: usernames, email addresses, encrypted passwords (using bcrypt or SHA1 with salting), and security questions and answers (encrypted).

In 2014, hackers launched an even larger-scale intrusion, with Yahoo reporting that approximately one billion user accounts were accessed. This breach was more covert than the first, with the hackers likely operating undetected within the system for an extended period.

The breach affected all three billion Yahoo user accounts, making it one of the largest data breaches in history.
